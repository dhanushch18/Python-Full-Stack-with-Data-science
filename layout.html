London

London is the capital city of England. It is the most populous city in the United Kingdom, with a metropolitan area of over 13 million inhabitants.

Standing on the River Thames, London has been a major settlement for two millennia, its history going back to its founding by the Romans, who named it Londinium.

Delhi

Delhi, India’s capital territory, is a massive metropolitan area in the
country’s north. In Old Delhi, a neighborhood dating to the 1600s,
stands the imposing Mughal-era Red Fort, a symbol of India, and the sprawling Jama Masjid mosque, whose courtyard accommodates 25,000 people.

Paris

Paris, France's capital, is a major European city and a global center for art,
fashion, gastronomy and culture. Its 19th-century cityscape is crisscrossed
by wide boulevards and the River Seine. Beyond such landmarks as the Eiffel Tower
and the 12th-century, Gothic Notre-Dame cathedral, the city is known for its
cafe culture and designer boutiques along the Rue du Faubourg Saint-Honoré.
